## 1. Cycle-Peak P/E Methodology

### Core Principle
Semi equipment trades at HIGH P/E at trough (on depressed earnings), LOW P/E at peak (on high earnings). Always value on peak earnings.

### Cycle Position Indicators

| Indicator | Trough | Mid-Cycle | Peak |
|-----------|--------|-----------|------|
| Customer capex | Cutting 20%+ | Flat to moderate | +30%+ YoY |
| Equipment lead times | Short (<3 mo) | Normal (6 mo) | Extended (12+ mo) |
| Fab utilization | <70% | 75-85% | >90% |
| Memory spot prices | Below curve | On curve | Above curve |
| Inventory at customers | Elevated | Normal | Lean |

### Multiple Selection

| Scenario | P/E Multiple | When to Apply |
|----------|--------------|---------------|
| Trough valuation | 50-60x | On depressed earnings |
| Mid-cycle | 35-40x | Recovery phase |
| Peak valuation | 25-35x | On peak earnings (TARGET) |

## Scenario Analysis Template

### Bull Case (+20-30% upside)
- Logic/foundry capex exceeds guidance
- HBM/DRAM spend higher than expected
- High NA adoption accelerates
- Service margins expand
- Multiple: 35-40x peak EPS

### Base Case
- Customer capex per guidance
- Normal cycle progression
- Gradual China transition
- Multiple: 30-32x peak EPS

### Bear Case (-20-30% downside)
- Demand shortfall, cycle extends
- Customer capex cuts
- China revenue collapse
- Multiple: 20-25x on lower peak EPS

## Red Flags

| Warning Sign | Interpretation |
|--------------|----------------|
| Order intake declining QoQ | Cycle turning |
| Lead times shortening | Demand softening |
| Customer pushing out deliveries | Capex cuts coming |
| Inventory building | Demand slowdown |
| Gross margin missing | Mix/pricing pressure |
